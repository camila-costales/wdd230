El Panecillo

The panecillo translates to “small piece of bread” in Spanish—is a small volcanic peak in central Quito. Originally called “Yavirac” by the indigenous people of Ecuador, the hill once housed a beautiful sun temple at its peak before being destroyed by Spanish conquistadores. Carolina Park

Parque La Carolina is a 165.5-acre (670,000 m2) park in the centre of the Quito central business district, bordered by the avenues Río Amazonas, de los Shyris, Naciones Unidas, Eloy Alfaro, and de la República. Iñaquito metro station is in the northern part of the park, whereas La Carolina metro station is in the southern part. Quito's Food

In Ecuadors highlands, you can find a huge diversity of traditional cuisine, such as soups, main dishes, and desserts. The traditional dishes of the highlands are pork and beef-based, combined with potatoes and corn grains, among other things. Population

Ecuador has a population of approximately 17.6 million people. It is a culturally diverse country with a majority of Mestizos, along with significant Indigenous and Afro-Ecuadorian communities. Major cities like Guayaquil and Quito experience high population density. Ecuador is known for its cultural diversity, with various ethnic groups comprising the population. The majority of Ecuadorians are Mestizos, a mix of Indigenous and European heritage. Indigenous communities, such as the Quechua and Shuar, have a significant presence in the country. Afro-Ecuadorians, of African descent, also contribute to the cultural fabric of Ecuador.
